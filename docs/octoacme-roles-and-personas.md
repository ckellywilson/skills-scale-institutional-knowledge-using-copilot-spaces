# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee release planning, coordination, and deployment activities. They ensure that software releases are delivered on time, meet quality standards, and are properly communicated to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases across development cycles
- Coordinate with developers, QA, and stakeholders for release readiness
- Manage release branches, versioning, and changelog documentation
- Oversee deployment processes and verify successful releases
- Maintain rollback procedures and incident response coordination
- Track release metrics and identify process improvements

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases on schedule
- Minimize production incidents and deployment failures
- Improve release process efficiency and automation
- Ensure clear communication and documentation for all releases

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Product and Engineering leads
- Pre-release readiness reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Post-release status updates and retrospectives
- Release notes and deployment announcements

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align release schedules with project milestones and communicate risks
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freeze, branch management, and deployment readiness
- **QA Analysts**: Verify release criteria are met and coordinate final testing
- **Communications Lead**: Partner on release announcements and stakeholder notifications
- **Product Managers**: Validate feature completeness and prioritize release scope

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads manage project communications across all stakeholder groups. They ensure consistent messaging, maintain documentation standards, and keep everyone informed about project status, decisions, and changes.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute communication plans for projects
- Create and distribute regular status updates to stakeholders
- Maintain project documentation and knowledge repositories
- Coordinate announcements for releases, changes, and incidents
- Facilitate information flow between teams and leadership
- Ensure communication standards and templates are followed

### Goals
- Maintain transparency and alignment across all stakeholders
- Reduce information gaps and miscommunication
- Establish consistent communication patterns and cadences
- Improve accessibility and discoverability of project information

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder status reports and newsletters
- Project kickoff and milestone announcements
- Documentation reviews and updates
- Escalation and incident communications

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on project status, risks, and milestone communications
- **Product Managers**: Align on feature messaging and customer-facing communications
- **Release Manager**: Partner on release notes, deployment schedules, and go-live announcements
- **Customer Liaison**: Ensure customer feedback is documented and shared with the team
- **All Roles**: Serve as central point for communication standards and distribution

---

## QA Analyst

### Role Summary
QA Analysts are responsible for ensuring software quality through test planning, execution, and defect management. They validate that features meet acceptance criteria and advocate for quality standards throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design test cases and test plans based on acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated tests across different environments
- Identify, document, and track defects through resolution
- Verify bug fixes and regression testing
- Collaborate on quality metrics and testing strategy
- Participate in requirements review to ensure testability

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Advocate for quality throughout the development process

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups reporting on testing progress and blockers
- Bug reports with clear reproduction steps and severity
- Test case reviews and coverage discussions
- Quality metrics in sprint reviews and retrospectives

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Report defects, verify fixes, and collaborate on testability improvements
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and validate feature behavior
- **Project Managers**: Report on testing status, risks, and quality metrics
- **Release Manager**: Confirm release readiness and coordinate final verification tests
- **Customer Liaison**: Incorporate real-world usage patterns into test scenarios

---

## Customer Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Liaisons serve as the primary interface between the project team and customers. They gather feedback, communicate customer needs to the team, and ensure the customer perspective is represented in product decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback and feature requests
- Represent customer needs in planning and prioritization discussions
- Communicate project updates and changes to customers
- Conduct user research and validation sessions
- Escalate customer issues and advocate for customer experience
- Document customer insights and usage patterns

### Goals
- Ensure customer voice is heard in product decisions
- Build strong relationships and trust with customer base
- Reduce friction between customer expectations and product delivery
- Improve customer satisfaction and product-market fit

### Typical Communication
- Regular customer check-ins and feedback sessions
- Customer advisory board meetings
- Feature validation and user acceptance testing
- Customer-facing release notes and training materials

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer insights, validate priorities, and influence roadmap decisions
- **Developers**: Provide real-world usage context and help troubleshoot customer issues
- **QA Analysts**: Share customer-reported issues and usage patterns for test planning
- **Communications Lead**: Coordinate customer-facing announcements and documentation
- **Metrics/Analytics Owner**: Correlate customer feedback with usage data and trends

---

## Metrics/Analytics Owner

### Role Summary
Metrics/Analytics Owners track project and product performance through data collection, analysis, and reporting. They provide data-driven insights to inform decisions, measure success, and identify opportunities for improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs) for projects
- Build and maintain dashboards for project and product metrics
- Analyze trends, patterns, and anomalies in data
- Provide data-driven recommendations for improvements
- Conduct A/B tests and experiments to validate hypotheses
- Report on success metrics defined in project goals

### Goals
- Enable data-informed decision making across the team
- Measure and communicate impact of product changes
- Identify opportunities for optimization and improvement
- Establish reliable metrics and monitoring practices

### Typical Communication
- Weekly or sprint-based metrics reviews with project teams
- Data analysis reports for leadership and stakeholders
- Experiment results and recommendations
- Dashboard reviews and metric definition discussions

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Define success metrics, validate feature impact, and inform prioritization
- **Project Managers**: Track project health metrics and identify risks early
- **Developers**: Instrument code for observability and collaborate on technical metrics
- **Customer Liaison**: Correlate quantitative data with qualitative customer feedback
- **Release Manager**: Monitor release quality metrics and deployment success rates

## Role Interaction Matrix

Understanding how these personas collaborate is essential for effective project execution. Below is a summary of key collaboration touchpoints:

| Interaction | Primary Activities |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Project Manager ↔ Release Manager** | Release planning, timeline coordination, risk management |
| **Product Manager ↔ Customer Liaison** | Customer insights, feature validation, roadmap prioritization |
| **Developers ↔ QA Analyst** | Defect triage, test automation, acceptance criteria validation |
| **Release Manager ↔ Communications Lead** | Release notes, deployment announcements, stakeholder notifications |
| **Metrics/Analytics Owner ↔ Product Manager** | Success metrics definition, feature impact analysis, A/B testing |
| **QA Analyst ↔ Customer Liaison** | Real-world test scenarios, customer-reported issue reproduction |
| **Communications Lead ↔ All Roles** | Status updates, documentation standards, information dissemination |
| **Project Manager ↔ All Roles** | Coordination, risk escalation, resource allocation, status tracking |
